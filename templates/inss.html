FAQ

Here are some questions you can use to assess stress levels in students:

Q1, General Feelings:

  • 1. How often do you feel overwhelmed by your schoolwork? (Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never)
  • 2. On a scale of 1 (not at all stressed) to 5 (extremely stressed), how stressed do you feel right now?
  • 3. In the past week, how often have you felt anxious or irritable? (Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never)

Q2, Sleep and Physical Symptoms:

  • 1. How many hours of sleep do you typically get per night?
  • 2. Have you noticed any changes in your appetite or eating habits recently? (Increased, Decreased, No Change)
  • 3. Are you experiencing any physical symptoms like headaches, stomachaches, or muscle tension? (Select all that apply)

Q3, Thoughts and Behaviors:

  • 1. Do you find it difficult to concentrate on your schoolwork? (Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never)
  • 2. Have you been feeling more negative or hopeless lately? (Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never)
  • 3. How often do you procrastinate on schoolwork? (Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never)
  • 4. Do you find yourself withdrawing from social activities or hobbies you used to enjoy? (Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never)

Q4, Academic Pressure:

  • 1. How much pressure do you feel to succeed academically? (A lot, Some, A little, Not much)
  • 2. Do you feel like you have to meet high expectations from your parents/teachers/yourself? (Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never)
  • 3. How often do you procrastinate on schoolwork? (Always, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never)
  • 4. How confident do you feel in your ability to manage your workload? (Very confident, Somewhat confident, Not very confident, Not confident at all)
